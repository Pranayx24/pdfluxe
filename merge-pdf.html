Advanced Tips & Best Practices

To get the absolute best results when compiling PDF documents, keep these best practices in mind:

  • Standardize Page Sizes: If you are merging PDFs with vastly different dimensions (like A4 and Letter size), the resulting file will maintain those distinct sizes. If consistency is required, adjust your documents beforehand.
  • Flatten Annotations: If your original PDFs contain interactive form fields or unflattened comments, they might behave unexpectedly after a merge. It is best to print to PDF first to flatten visual elements.
  • File Naming: Before you merge, rename your individual files with numbering (e.g., "01-Cover.pdf", "02-Body.pdf"). This helps you organize them much faster when uploading them into the grid.

Common Problems When Merging PDFs (And Solutions)

Even the simplest tools can run into edge cases. Here’s how to solve common hurdles you might face while combining documents:

Problem: "One of my files is password protected."
Solution: Before you can merge an encrypted file, you must remove its security restrictions. You can easily do this by using our Unlock PDF tool to decrypt the file, after which it will merge perfectly.

Problem: "The final merged PDF is too large to send via email."
Solution: A common constraint with email providers is the 25MB attachment limit. After merging, immediately route your new document through our Compress PDF utility to drastically reduce its file size while maintaining pristine quality.

Problem: "My pages are upside down after merging."
Solution: If the source files were scanned incorrectly, their rotation will persist. Utilize our Rotate PDF feature to fix the page orientations before executing the final merge.
